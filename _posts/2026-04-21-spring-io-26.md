Spring I/O is something I discovered on YouTube a few years ago and have been a huge fan of ever since. I used to watch each and every published video, and stay amazed with the quality and the general sense of community. Year after year I was looking forward to seeing those familiar faces, I could recognize almost all of them by names, last names, careers, domains, and this feeling of familiarity was born. 

This year I decided to invest in this event and attend it in person (supported and almost forced by [Tornike](https://www.linkedin.com/in/tornikeo/)). It was not a small decision, given that I had to take care of a lot things out of my own pocket, but let me tell you a spoiler - it was all worth it.

Here are some highlights, conversations, impressions from the conference that I could not hold to myself only, right? 

## April 13

{% include carousel.html items=page.community_gathering label="Conference Venue" %}

Conference kicked off with a beautiful community night gathering at a local bar. We enjoyed some drinks and had warm conversations in the amazing, unofficial setting. This really helped to break the ice and bring people together. I would highly recommend anyone to attend any such icebreaking events if they get a chance. Who knows, you might even say hi to those you look up to 😏

On that night I met in person wonderful people from Switzerland ([Simon](https://www.linkedin.com/in/simonmartinelli), [Patrick](https://www.linkedin.com/in/patbaumgartner)), Germany ([Philip](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rieckpil/)), Lithuania ([Ina](https://www.linkedin.com/in/inabumstein)), Austria (Philippe, Dusan, Denys, Dominique), Norway (Anil) and many many more. Had a wonderful time discussing the future of AI and humanity alongside it (because who can avoid that at a tech conference?). These people stayed as anchors for me throught the whole conference, helping me navigate the situation and enjoy my time there.

## April 15

<figure>
    <img src="https://i.imgur.com/iDenEvV.jpeg" alt="IntelliJ Community Run" style="width:100%">

Not bragging at all, but this was a special day as I took part in the community run event organized by IntelliJ itself (not kidding) - I even got the cutest limited running apparel. I was really stressed beforehand, fearing that I might have embarrassed myself and be crawling the whole time (it was 5-6 km, sweet spot for me, but "easy" by others' assessments). 

{% include carousel.html items=page.intellij_run label="Intellij Community Run" %}

We caught the early sunrise, ran through the area around the conference venue and started the day energized. It was the first time I had ran in a group and my running buddy (we were mostly running in pairs or a few people per line) Mauricio Salatino kept a perfect company for me and I almost did not feel any fatigue at all. After a quick shower back at the hotel, day continued with more interesting sessions. 

{% include carousel.html items=page.pollack_workshop label="Mark Pollack's Workshop" %}

Mark Pollack delivered a very helpful workshop that hopefully reminded everyone that vibe coding is not a sin, but carefully engineering the harness can give really valuable results - on the example of a review judge (?).

[pic]


- First time ever on such big scale conference in my field.
- A lot of people asked me if it was worth attending - absolutely. If it was worth overcoming the embarrassment and talk with completely unfamiliar people - more than absolutely, as that's how you meet new friends and allies and build a powerful network. 
- A lot of sessions caught my attention - superstarts Josh Long, Simon Martinelli, Victor Rentea, Christian Tzolov... Bit more on the specific sessions below. 
- Met wonderful people from Lithuania, Austria, Norway, Germany... the whole world.

I hope you can probably tell how much fun I had. Not only fun but very very important opportunity to shake hands with the leading experts in the field, meeting a lot of people - experienced or beginners, from the whole Europe. For those two days, that place felt very cozy and comfortable like home - full of inspiring conversations and discussions buzzing around.

Next year Spring I/O is set to be held in Valencia for the first time ever. Fingers crossed, you will see a new version of this diary from me around that time.
